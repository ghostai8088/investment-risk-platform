## Key Learnings
- **The s2 decoupling (RS-1 HIGH-value catch):** `std_errors` in the OLS kernel consume `s2` — so an EWMA convention must KEEP classical `s2 = ss_res/(n−m)` for the coefficient std-errors and dispatch a SEPARATE `residual_var` onto `residual_stdev` only. This is the vendor-standard separation (Barra/Axioma take factor inference from the OLS regression, model specific risk separately), not a compromise.
- **`sole_declared`'s absent/ambiguous conflation is a trap when a literal is OPTIONAL:** it returns None for BOTH zero and >1 matches. A grandfather gate (absent ⇒ default) must COUNT rows explicitly — 0 ⇒ default, >1 ⇒ fail-closed — or ambiguity silently collapses into the grandfather. This was RS-1's adversarial HIGH; folded into DS-2 from birth.
- **Closure-by-supersession can silently DROP unremediated disclosures:** RS-1's fresh TRIGGERED conditions dropped the MF-1 frequency-conversion caveat (nothing had remediated it). Rule: restate EVERY surviving caveat in the fresh conditions.
- **The age-gate purpose check bites ANY new snapshot-purpose citation** into promote/total-VaR (BT-2's purpose fold has teeth): admitting a new purpose is a deliberate, recorded Part-5.5 deviation. RS-1's `RESIDUAL_SHRINKAGE_INPUT` had to be added to `_MEASURABLE_ESTIMATE_PURPOSES` (as-of = stalest cohort member's span end — conservative).
- **The Okunev-White lag-i algebra, settled by derivation + executed proof:** the per-pass coefficient c_i is the '−' root of `ρ_i·c² − (1+ρ_2i)·c + ρ_i = 0`; a lag-i filter zeroes the lag-i autocorrelation, a lag-1 filter with the same c does NOT (the vendor's rendered lag was a transcription artifact). Vieta: roots are reciprocals ⇒ exactly one |c|≤1 root for disc>0; at disc=0 with ρ_i<0 a c=−1 double root arrives (harmless, unreachable).
- **The Bartlett band direction was BACKWARDS in my first DS-2 draft** (planning verifier's R1): 1/√n is CONSERVATIVE (overstates SE) under AR(1) at lag 1 — the exact-AR1 band is narrower. The real unstated hazard is the small-sample DOWNWARD bias of ρ̂₁ (Kendall/Marriott-Pope ≈ −(1+4φ)/n) ⇒ α̂ biased UPWARD.
- **Fixture-search discipline:** an AR(1)-shaped generator keeps ρ̂₂≈ρ̂₁² so OW discriminants stay positive; a flat-ρ̂₂ cycle makes (1+ρ₂)²<4ρ₁² and the OW filter refuses. Always search for a fixture admissible for BOTH conventions.
