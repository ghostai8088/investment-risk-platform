## 1. The thesis

**Unified public + private investment risk, done with best-in-class mathematics, on an AI-ready governed platform** —
not replication of incumbent vendors.

## 2. The three pillars

### 2.1 Public + private integration (the destination)
The hard problem is not co-locating asset types; it is that private assets **understate their own risk**:
appraisal-based quarterly marks smooth away volatility and lag market moves. First-class treatment requires real
transformation mathematics — desmoothing appraisal returns, mapping private holdings onto observable risk factors
(proxying), commitment/capital-call handling — with the introduced uncertainty stated honestly, not hidden.
**Sequencing consequence (recorded, not aspirational):** the public-market factor/covariance/VaR machinery being
built now IS the substrate private-asset proxies project onto — the current roadmap is the prerequisite for the
thesis, and private assets are its destination, not "a later theme."

### 2.2 Best-in-class mathematics (the bar)
Calculation AND transformation/proxy math is benchmarked against the **academic and regulatory state of the art**
(published literature, regulatory standards), not against vendor behavior. Every methodology slice carries a cited
external-benchmark section in its decision record; deviations from established practice are fixed or justified in
writing. Assumptions and limitations stay first-class in every methodology document (the existing standard).

### 2.3 AI-ready architecture (the enabler)
"Cutting edge" here means **agent-consumable and verifiable**, not exotic. The properties that make deep AI
integration safe are the platform's existing governance obsessions, now named as deliberate architectural
principles to be preserved and extended:
- every number traces to its inputs (lineage) and reproduces on demand (snapshot + run + registered model binding);
- every action is attributed, audited, and permissioned deny-by-default;
- every capability is exposed through governed APIs (no side doors), and every governance artifact (model
  registry, assumptions, limitations, methodology docs, decision records) is machine-readable;
- the base technology stays deliberately boring; the innovation budget is spent on the governance/reproducibility
  layer — which is exactly what AI agents require to operate inside a risk platform without becoming an
  unauditable black box.

## 3. What this thesis explicitly rejects
Vendor feature-parity as a goal; opaque private-asset treatment (smoothed marks passed through as truth); exotic
infrastructure adopted for novelty; AI integration that bypasses the governance rails.

## 4. Standing obligations (wired into the roadmap)
1. Methodology slices include a **cited external-benchmark research section** (sources + dates) — extended
   explicitly to transformation/proxy mathematics when private-asset slices begin.
2. Wave-close re-baselines include an **outward-facing benchmark review** (architecture, data design,
   methodologies, security, engineering practice vs. current published best practice).
3. Every wave-close re-baseline **evaluates progress toward the public+private destination** — beginning with the
   Wave-1 close, whose Wave-2 proposal must explicitly weigh pulling private-asset foundations (captured-data
   slices + the first proxy-methodology family) forward against the remaining public-market legs.
