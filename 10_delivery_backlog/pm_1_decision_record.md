## Part 2 — External benchmark research (roadmap Part 4 rule 6; sources + dates checked 2026-07-09)

| Source | What it prescribes | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| **GIPS Standards 2020 (CFA Institute), provisions 2.A.2/2.A.19–24** | Total-return TWR that adjusts for external cash flows; valuation at the time of "large" external flows; at minimum monthly valuation; geometric linking of sub-period returns; Modified Dietz permitted between valuation points. | ADOPTED structurally: chain-linked TWR with Dietz within sub-periods; **valuation-at-flow is the caller's run-boundary lever** (a run at the flow date = pure TWR), which is GIPS's own hierarchy. **Deviation (declared):** no "large flow" threshold parameter in v1 — EVERY boundary is caller-chosen; no composite construction (firm-level GIPS scope, not platform scope). |
| **Dietz (1966); Modified Dietz per GIPS glossary / CFA curriculum** | `r = (EMV − BMV − F) / (BMV + Σ w·F)`, day-weighted flows. | ADOPTED verbatim as the within-sub-period estimator; calendar-day weights, end-of-day flow convention — both DECLARED assumptions (version identity). |
| **Bacon, *Practical Portfolio Performance Measurement & Attribution* (2nd ed., 2008)** | The TWR/MWR taxonomy; Dietz denominator pathologies (small/negative average capital); linking conventions. | ADOPTED: the `BMV ≤ 0` / denominator `≤ 0` REFUSAL gates come straight from the recorded pathology (a Dietz return over near-zero capital is meaningless — fail closed, never publish garbage). |
| **Feibel, *Investment Performance Measurement* (2003)** | Return-frequency and linking error taxonomy; gross-vs-net conventions. | ADOPTED: gross-of-fees v1 DECLARED (no fee capture exists); geometric linking only (no arithmetic linking). |
| **GIPS 2020 provisions on money-weighted returns (2.A.25+)** | Since-inception MWR/IRR for closed-end / committed-capital vehicles. | DEFERRED TO PA-0 by design — IRR is the private-asset measure; building it belongs with commitments/calls/distributions capture (REQ-PRV-002), not here. Recording the seam now is the thesis's dependency-order principle. |

**Net deviations from the cited state of the art, all declared:** (1) gross-of-fees only; (2) captured-holdings book (no cash ledger — OD-K, the loud one); (3) no large-flow threshold auto-valuation (caller-supplied boundaries); (4) no composites; (5) daily/period frequency = caller's run cadence, unannualized.
