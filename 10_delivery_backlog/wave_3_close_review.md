## Part 4 — The destination check (thesis §2.1/§2.2) and where Wave 4 points

Wave 1 built the public-market risk substrate; Wave 2 built the return-series spine + began the private-asset thread (PA-0). **Wave 3 arrived at the thesis §2.1 destination:** a private holding now flows capture → desmooth → proxy → factor risk → VaR and carries honest, factor-based risk, proven byte-exact against its public equivalent. The honest reading of "arrived in v1 form": the *pipeline* exists, but two of its links are still stubbed with the honest-default choice — proxy **weights are captured, not estimated** (so PA-1's desmoothed series is not yet an input to anything), and the factor universe is **CURRENCY-only** (so specific risk is still 0 and the demonstration book is deliberately simple). - **(A) Deepen the private-asset payload toward best-in-class (thesis §2.2):** regression-estimated weights v2 (the loop-closer — makes desmoothing *matter* downstream), then proxy-aware active-risk / residual-variance. Highest thesis leverage; stays in the differentiation lane.
- **(B) Pivot to the nearest supervisory gap (RTM-P7 validation workflow):** the model-governance ingredient BT-1 already feeds; well-staged, outward-credibility value, but not thesis-differentiating.
- **(C) Breadth (credit/counterparty RTM-P3, limits/breach/SoD RTM-P6, reporting RTM-P8):** new domains; widens the platform but defers deepening the payload just reached.

Deferring nothing is not on the table — the register is healthy and trigger-based. The recommendation (Part 5) is **A, behind one small hygiene slice**, because the thesis is the platform's whole reason for being and Wave 3 left its most valuable link (regression weights) one slice away from closing — but this is a Tier-3 direction call and the fork is put to the user honestly as OQ-W3C-2.
