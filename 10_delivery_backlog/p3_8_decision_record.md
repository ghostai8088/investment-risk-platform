## Part 2 — External benchmark research (roadmap Part 4 rule 6; sources checked 2026-07-10)

| Source | What it says | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| **ESMA Guidelines on ETFs and other UCITS issues (ESMA/2012/832, rev. 2014/937)** | Ex-post tracking error = "the volatility of the difference between the return of the fund and the return of the benchmark"; tracking DIFFERENCE = the difference between the fund's and the benchmark's returns over a period — two DISTINCT disclosure figures. | ADOPTED as the defining pair: TE = stdev of per-period active returns; TD = compounded-return difference over the span. **Deviation (declared):** v1 is UNANNUALIZED (UCITS disclosure TE is typically annualized) — the methodology doc labels the figures so they cannot be conflated with the disclosure numbers (the P3-7 ex-ante labeling precedent); annualization is a deferred declared transform. |
| **Grinold, R. & Kahn, R. (2000), *Active Portfolio Management*, 2nd ed.** | IR = active return ÷ tracking error; realized IR uses realized active-return mean over realized TE. | ADOPTED: `IR = mean(a_i)/TE`, per-period basis, unannualized. TE→0 ⇒ IR undefined ⇒ row omitted (not fabricated, not refused — a perfectly-tracking book is a legitimate input). |
| **Bacon, C., *Practical Portfolio Performance Measurement and Attribution* (2nd ed.); CFA Institute curriculum** | Ex-post TE = SAMPLE standard deviation (n−1) of arithmetic active returns; arithmetic vs geometric excess-return variants exist. | ADOPTED: arithmetic `a_i = r_p,i − r_b,i` (the standard TE input; the geometric-excess variant is a declared non-goal v1); n−1 sample stdev (the P3-4 covariance unbiased-sample precedent); ≥2 observations required for TE. |
| **GIPS 2020 (CFA Institute)** | Firms must present benchmark returns alongside portfolio/composite returns; the benchmark and its variant (price/total/net) must be disclosed. | GROUNDS OD-E: the `return_basis` choice is explicit, echoed on every row, and the gross-vs-net comparability caveat is recorded — never silently adjusted. |

**Net declared deviations:** (1) unannualized (vs the UCITS disclosure convention); (2) arithmetic active returns (geometric excess deferred); (3) sub-period grain = the PM-1 boundaries (not calendar-regular periods); (4) benchmark compounding over AVAILABLE pinned rows (no trading-calendar completeness validation — loud limitation); (5) single benchmark per run.

## Part 3 — Limitations carried forward + out of scope (recorded)

**The PM-1 OD-K limitation, NAMED AGAIN as obliged:** the portfolio side measures the **CAPTURED-HOLDINGS book** (no cash ledger — uncaptured dividend/interest income is invisible to MV, understating the portfolio return). **This propagates INTO every P3-8 number**: active returns, TD, TE, and IR inherit the understatement bias against a TOTAL-return benchmark. Carried as a first-class `model_limitation` + methodology-doc Known Limitation; the mitigation remains operational (capture the cash), never imputation.

**Out of scope (recorded deferrals):** annualization/√T scaling; relative VaR (the CESR/10-788 seam, unchanged); active share; performance attribution (CAP-20.3, its own GIPS-grade slice); geometric excess returns; multi-benchmark comparisons; LOG return_type; trading-calendar completeness validation for benchmark series; net-of-fees (no fee capture); any change to the P2-7 capture surfaces; NO new permission/audit mint; no BYPASSRLS/hybrid; `audit/service.py` FROZEN.
